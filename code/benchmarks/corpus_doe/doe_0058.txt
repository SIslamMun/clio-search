The University of Nebraska-Lincoln (UNL) Meteodrone (model SSE MM-641) is a small (1.1 kg GTOW, ~40 cm across) hexcopter UAS manufactured by Meteomatics. Maximum endurance is approximately 30 min and estimated maximum operating altitude of 1500 m.&nbsp; Profiles can be executed at ascent/descent rates up to 10 m/s.&nbsp; The Meteodrone is capable of measuring temperature, relative humidity, wind velocity and GPS position time-stamped with GPS time.&nbsp; Temperature and dual relative humidity sensors are shielded from solar exposure by a housing with a vertically oriented air intake and hoirizontal exhaust.&nbsp; Sensor aspiration is driven by rotorwash across the horizontal exhaust.&nbsp; In its current configuration data are recorded only when executing vertical profiles.&nbsp; Attitude and position are updated at a data rate of 20 Hz while meteorological observations are updated at 10 Hz.&nbsp;
