Calorimetry Report: Combustion Enthalpy

Experiment: CAL-427
Calorimeter type: isothermal titration (ITC)

Sample: 864.1 mg (0.754 g) of
benzoic acid
in 81 mL of water

Measurement:
- Initial temperature: 23.679 degC
- Final temperature: 34.573 degC
- Temperature rise: 9.753 degC
- Calorimeter constant: 428.5 J/degC
- Measured heat: 3.52 kJ
- Molar enthalpy: -480.2 kJ/mol

Using the relation q = m * c * Delta_T and correcting for heat losses, the
enthalpy of combustion was determined.
The ideal gas law PV = nRT was used to correct for gas-phase products where
applicable. At the reaction pressure of 102.6 kPa, the molar
volume correction is negligible.

Uncertainty analysis: the combined standard uncertainty is 1.8%
(k=2), dominated by the temperature measurement uncertainty of 0.016 degC.
Replicate measurements (n=5): mean = -293.7 kJ/mol,
s.d. = 7.7 kJ/mol. Duration of each run: 20 min.
