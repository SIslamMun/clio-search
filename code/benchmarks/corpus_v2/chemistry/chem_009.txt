Calorimetry Report: Dissolution Enthalpy

Experiment: CAL-409
Calorimeter type: isothermal titration (ITC)

Sample: 666.8 mg (0.268 g) of
urea
in 109 mL of NaOH solution

Measurement:
- Initial temperature: 24.456 degC
- Final temperature: 26.171 degC
- Temperature rise: 6.330 degC
- Calorimeter constant: 477.8 J/degC
- Measured heat: 7.34 kJ
- Molar enthalpy: -407.4 kJ/mol

Using the relation q = m * c * Delta_T and correcting for heat losses, the
enthalpy of neutralization was determined.
The ideal gas law PV = nRT was used to correct for gas-phase products where
applicable. At the reaction pressure of 102.2 kPa, the molar
volume correction is negligible.

Uncertainty analysis: the combined standard uncertainty is 0.7%
(k=2), dominated by the temperature measurement uncertainty of 0.007 degC.
Replicate measurements (n=4): mean = -335.4 kJ/mol,
s.d. = 2.8 kJ/mol. Duration of each run: 8 min.
