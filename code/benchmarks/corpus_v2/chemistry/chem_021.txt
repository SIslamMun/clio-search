Calorimetry Report: Mixing Enthalpy

Experiment: CAL-421
Calorimeter type: differential scanning (DSC)

Sample: 591.7 mg (0.255 g) of
urea
in 193 mL of water

Measurement:
- Initial temperature: 22.067 degC
- Final temperature: 24.743 degC
- Temperature rise: 3.360 degC
- Calorimeter constant: 408.0 J/degC
- Measured heat: 1.54 kJ
- Molar enthalpy: -144.8 kJ/mol

Using the relation q = m * c * Delta_T and correcting for heat losses, the
enthalpy of neutralization was determined.
The ideal gas law PV = nRT was used to correct for gas-phase products where
applicable. At the reaction pressure of 100.1 kPa, the molar
volume correction is negligible.

Uncertainty analysis: the combined standard uncertainty is 2.3%
(k=2), dominated by the temperature measurement uncertainty of 0.017 degC.
Replicate measurements (n=6): mean = -184.2 kJ/mol,
s.d. = 1.7 kJ/mol. Duration of each run: 18 min.
