Calorimetry Report: Mixing Enthalpy

Experiment: CAL-415
Calorimeter type: isothermal titration (ITC)

Sample: 228.0 mg (0.669 g) of
urea
in 106 mL of water

Measurement:
- Initial temperature: 24.535 degC
- Final temperature: 33.681 degC
- Temperature rise: 1.838 degC
- Calorimeter constant: 503.1 J/degC
- Measured heat: 3.50 kJ
- Molar enthalpy: -78.6 kJ/mol

Using the relation q = m * c * Delta_T and correcting for heat losses, the
enthalpy of dissolution was determined.
The ideal gas law PV = nRT was used to correct for gas-phase products where
applicable. At the reaction pressure of 100.8 kPa, the molar
volume correction is negligible.

Uncertainty analysis: the combined standard uncertainty is 1.7%
(k=2), dominated by the temperature measurement uncertainty of 0.014 degC.
Replicate measurements (n=3): mean = -171.1 kJ/mol,
s.d. = 5.1 kJ/mol. Duration of each run: 22 min.
