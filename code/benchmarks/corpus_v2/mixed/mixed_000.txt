Wind Energy Assessment: Turbine Site 100

This multi-disciplinary report covers the aerodynamic, structural, and atmospheric
analysis for a proposed wind turbine installation.

Atmospheric conditions (annual averages):
- Mean wind speed at hub height (80 m): 7.8 m/s (18.9 km/h)
- Wind power density: 529 W/m^2
- Extreme gust (50-year return): 65 m/s
- Barometric pressure (site elevation 1604 m): 100.2 kPa
- Air density: 1.159 kg/m^3

Turbine specifications:
- Rotor diameter: 160 m (130610 mm)
- Blade length: 67 m
- Blade root thickness: 58 mm
- Tower height: 80 m
- Rated wind speed: 14.1 m/s
- Cut-in speed: 5 m/s

Structural analysis:
- Maximum blade root bending moment: 26.1 MN*m
- Blade tip deflection: 4.7 m (6837 mm)
- Tower base von Mises stress: 190 MPa (207499 kPa)
- Foundation design pressure: 309 kPa on soil

The power output P = 0.5 * rho * A * Cp * v^3 at rated conditions gives
approximately 4.7 MW. Annual energy production estimate:
7 GWh at a capacity factor of 41%.
Fatigue life assessment: 20+ years at 1.2e+07 cycles.
