Wind Energy Assessment: Turbine Site 108

This multi-disciplinary report covers the aerodynamic, structural, and atmospheric
analysis for a proposed wind turbine installation.

Atmospheric conditions (annual averages):
- Mean wind speed at hub height (80 m): 8.9 m/s (37.0 km/h)
- Wind power density: 590 W/m^2
- Extreme gust (50-year return): 43 m/s
- Barometric pressure (site elevation 757 m): 85.0 kPa
- Air density: 1.161 kg/m^3

Turbine specifications:
- Rotor diameter: 163 m (101944 mm)
- Blade length: 40 m
- Blade root thickness: 75 mm
- Tower height: 83 m
- Rated wind speed: 14.0 m/s
- Cut-in speed: 3 m/s

Structural analysis:
- Maximum blade root bending moment: 11.4 MN*m
- Blade tip deflection: 6.6 m (3124 mm)
- Tower base von Mises stress: 178 MPa (215574 kPa)
- Foundation design pressure: 354 kPa on soil

The power output P = 0.5 * rho * A * Cp * v^3 at rated conditions gives
approximately 5.1 MW. Annual energy production estimate:
15 GWh at a capacity factor of 32%.
Fatigue life assessment: 20+ years at 6.6e+08 cycles.
