Pipe Flow Experiment: Run PF-125

A fully developed turbulent pipe flow experiment was conducted in a 78 mm
inner diameter smooth pipe. The pipe length was 5.2 m, giving an L/D ratio
of 50, sufficient for fully developed conditions. Working fluid: water
at 24.4 degC (kinematic viscosity approximately 1.0e-6 m^2/s).

Flow conditions:
- Bulk velocity: 1.83 m/s
- Reynolds number: Re = 166120
- Volume flow rate: 4.49 L/s
- Inlet pressure: 485 kPa (gauge)
- Outlet pressure: 148 kPa (gauge)
- Pressure drop: 141.9 kPa over the test section

The pressure drop of 296763 Pa corresponds to a Darcy friction
factor of f = 0.0262, in good agreement with the Moody diagram
prediction. Wall shear stress was estimated at 71.7 Pa using the
relation tau_w = (delta_P * D) / (4 * L).

PIV measurements in a cross-sectional plane at x/D = 141 revealed
the expected logarithmic velocity profile. The centerline velocity was
4.69 m/s (17.8 km/h).
Turbulence intensity at the centerline: 7.1%.
