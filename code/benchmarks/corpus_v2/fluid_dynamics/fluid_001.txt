Turbulent Flow Simulation: Run T-2001

This simulation models turbulent flow through a converging nozzle at Reynolds
number Re = 43246. The inlet velocity was set to
23.7 m/s with a turbulence intensity of 5%.
The nozzle throat diameter was 17 mm, expanding to
50 mm at the outlet.

Boundary conditions: inlet pressure 397 kPa (gauge),
outlet pressure 100.5 kPa (atmospheric). The simulation ran
for 3.3 seconds of physical time using a time step of
0.0039 s. Peak velocity at the throat reached
63.3 m/s, consistent with the isentropic relation
v = sqrt(2 * delta_P / rho).

The pressure drop across the nozzle was 205.7 kPa,
corresponding to 201564 Pa. Grid convergence was verified
using three mesh levels (0.6 mm, 1.4 mm,
2.5 mm spacing). The k-epsilon turbulence model was employed with
standard wall functions. Total cell count: 4.9 million.

The throat velocity of 352 km/h indicates compressibility effects
may become relevant. Mach number at throat: 0.21.
