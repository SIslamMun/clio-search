Turbulent Flow Simulation: Run T-2006

This simulation models turbulent flow through a converging nozzle at Reynolds
number Re = 24869. The inlet velocity was set to
5.6 m/s with a turbulence intensity of 3%.
The nozzle throat diameter was 28 mm, expanding to
77 mm at the outlet.

Boundary conditions: inlet pressure 235 kPa (gauge),
outlet pressure 103.9 kPa (atmospheric). The simulation ran
for 3.8 seconds of physical time using a time step of
0.0015 s. Peak velocity at the throat reached
111.2 m/s, consistent with the isentropic relation
v = sqrt(2 * delta_P / rho).

The pressure drop across the nozzle was 308.2 kPa,
corresponding to 399589 Pa. Grid convergence was verified
using three mesh levels (0.4 mm, 0.9 mm,
1.6 mm spacing). The k-epsilon turbulence model was employed with
standard wall functions. Total cell count: 1.3 million.

The throat velocity of 437 km/h indicates compressibility effects
may become relevant. Mach number at throat: 0.50.
