Turbulent Flow Simulation: Run T-2011

This simulation models turbulent flow through a converging nozzle at Reynolds
number Re = 47967. The inlet velocity was set to
21.0 m/s with a turbulence intensity of 4%.
The nozzle throat diameter was 21 mm, expanding to
72 mm at the outlet.

Boundary conditions: inlet pressure 322 kPa (gauge),
outlet pressure 102.3 kPa (atmospheric). The simulation ran
for 3.8 seconds of physical time using a time step of
0.0012 s. Peak velocity at the throat reached
91.6 m/s, consistent with the isentropic relation
v = sqrt(2 * delta_P / rho).

The pressure drop across the nozzle was 239.3 kPa,
corresponding to 202058 Pa. Grid convergence was verified
using three mesh levels (0.4 mm, 1.4 mm,
2.0 mm spacing). The k-epsilon turbulence model was employed with
standard wall functions. Total cell count: 4.9 million.

The throat velocity of 304 km/h indicates compressibility effects
may become relevant. Mach number at throat: 0.29.
