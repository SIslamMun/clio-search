Turbulent Flow Simulation: Run T-2016

This simulation models turbulent flow through a converging nozzle at Reynolds
number Re = 83783. The inlet velocity was set to
12.5 m/s with a turbulence intensity of 9%.
The nozzle throat diameter was 24 mm, expanding to
67 mm at the outlet.

Boundary conditions: inlet pressure 494 kPa (gauge),
outlet pressure 102.9 kPa (atmospheric). The simulation ran
for 4.2 seconds of physical time using a time step of
0.0038 s. Peak velocity at the throat reached
118.8 m/s, consistent with the isentropic relation
v = sqrt(2 * delta_P / rho).

The pressure drop across the nozzle was 108.0 kPa,
corresponding to 242377 Pa. Grid convergence was verified
using three mesh levels (0.8 mm, 1.3 mm,
2.7 mm spacing). The k-epsilon turbulence model was employed with
standard wall functions. Total cell count: 3.1 million.

The throat velocity of 440 km/h indicates compressibility effects
may become relevant. Mach number at throat: 0.35.
