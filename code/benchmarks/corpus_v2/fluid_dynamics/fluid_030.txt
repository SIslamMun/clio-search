Pipe Flow Experiment: Run PF-130

A fully developed turbulent pipe flow experiment was conducted in a 70 mm
inner diameter smooth pipe. The pipe length was 7.9 m, giving an L/D ratio
of 38, sufficient for fully developed conditions. Working fluid: water
at 23.2 degC (kinematic viscosity approximately 1.0e-6 m^2/s).

Flow conditions:
- Bulk velocity: 2.31 m/s
- Reynolds number: Re = 66430
- Volume flow rate: 10.84 L/s
- Inlet pressure: 414 kPa (gauge)
- Outlet pressure: 178 kPa (gauge)
- Pressure drop: 50.6 kPa over the test section

The pressure drop of 298110 Pa corresponds to a Darcy friction
factor of f = 0.0220, in good agreement with the Moody diagram
prediction. Wall shear stress was estimated at 92.1 Pa using the
relation tau_w = (delta_P * D) / (4 * L).

PIV measurements in a cross-sectional plane at x/D = 108 revealed
the expected logarithmic velocity profile. The centerline velocity was
3.72 m/s (17.2 km/h).
Turbulence intensity at the centerline: 7.1%.
