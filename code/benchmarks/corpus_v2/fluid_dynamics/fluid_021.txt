Turbulent Flow Simulation: Run T-2021

This simulation models turbulent flow through a converging nozzle at Reynolds
number Re = 23178. The inlet velocity was set to
22.0 m/s with a turbulence intensity of 3%.
The nozzle throat diameter was 39 mm, expanding to
79 mm at the outlet.

Boundary conditions: inlet pressure 247 kPa (gauge),
outlet pressure 103.3 kPa (atmospheric). The simulation ran
for 4.5 seconds of physical time using a time step of
0.0026 s. Peak velocity at the throat reached
115.1 m/s, consistent with the isentropic relation
v = sqrt(2 * delta_P / rho).

The pressure drop across the nozzle was 326.8 kPa,
corresponding to 186174 Pa. Grid convergence was verified
using three mesh levels (0.4 mm, 0.8 mm,
2.7 mm spacing). The k-epsilon turbulence model was employed with
standard wall functions. Total cell count: 3.4 million.

The throat velocity of 264 km/h indicates compressibility effects
may become relevant. Mach number at throat: 0.41.
