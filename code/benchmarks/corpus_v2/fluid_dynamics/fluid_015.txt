Pipe Flow Experiment: Run PF-115

A fully developed turbulent pipe flow experiment was conducted in a 28 mm
inner diameter smooth pipe. The pipe length was 4.0 m, giving an L/D ratio
of 87, sufficient for fully developed conditions. Working fluid: water
at 21.3 degC (kinematic viscosity approximately 1.0e-6 m^2/s).

Flow conditions:
- Bulk velocity: 2.56 m/s
- Reynolds number: Re = 163927
- Volume flow rate: 17.87 L/s
- Inlet pressure: 199 kPa (gauge)
- Outlet pressure: 193 kPa (gauge)
- Pressure drop: 102.4 kPa over the test section

The pressure drop of 123957 Pa corresponds to a Darcy friction
factor of f = 0.0335, in good agreement with the Moody diagram
prediction. Wall shear stress was estimated at 85.0 Pa using the
relation tau_w = (delta_P * D) / (4 * L).

PIV measurements in a cross-sectional plane at x/D = 109 revealed
the expected logarithmic velocity profile. The centerline velocity was
3.34 m/s (12.1 km/h).
Turbulence intensity at the centerline: 5.4%.
