Turbulent Flow Simulation: Run T-2031

This simulation models turbulent flow through a converging nozzle at Reynolds
number Re = 41051. The inlet velocity was set to
12.5 m/s with a turbulence intensity of 3%.
The nozzle throat diameter was 32 mm, expanding to
49 mm at the outlet.

Boundary conditions: inlet pressure 290 kPa (gauge),
outlet pressure 100.3 kPa (atmospheric). The simulation ran
for 4.8 seconds of physical time using a time step of
0.0045 s. Peak velocity at the throat reached
141.2 m/s, consistent with the isentropic relation
v = sqrt(2 * delta_P / rho).

The pressure drop across the nozzle was 287.8 kPa,
corresponding to 228160 Pa. Grid convergence was verified
using three mesh levels (0.5 mm, 1.5 mm,
2.9 mm spacing). The k-epsilon turbulence model was employed with
standard wall functions. Total cell count: 3.5 million.

The throat velocity of 463 km/h indicates compressibility effects
may become relevant. Mach number at throat: 0.26.
