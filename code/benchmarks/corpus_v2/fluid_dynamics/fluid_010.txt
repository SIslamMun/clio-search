Pipe Flow Experiment: Run PF-110

A fully developed turbulent pipe flow experiment was conducted in a 38 mm
inner diameter smooth pipe. The pipe length was 7.4 m, giving an L/D ratio
of 183, sufficient for fully developed conditions. Working fluid: water
at 20.4 degC (kinematic viscosity approximately 1.0e-6 m^2/s).

Flow conditions:
- Bulk velocity: 4.66 m/s
- Reynolds number: Re = 15798
- Volume flow rate: 16.52 L/s
- Inlet pressure: 160 kPa (gauge)
- Outlet pressure: 105 kPa (gauge)
- Pressure drop: 196.0 kPa over the test section

The pressure drop of 177264 Pa corresponds to a Darcy friction
factor of f = 0.0345, in good agreement with the Moody diagram
prediction. Wall shear stress was estimated at 77.9 Pa using the
relation tau_w = (delta_P * D) / (4 * L).

PIV measurements in a cross-sectional plane at x/D = 92 revealed
the expected logarithmic velocity profile. The centerline velocity was
4.05 m/s (11.8 km/h).
Turbulence intensity at the centerline: 6.1%.
