Pipe Flow Experiment: Run PF-120

A fully developed turbulent pipe flow experiment was conducted in a 74 mm
inner diameter smooth pipe. The pipe length was 8.2 m, giving an L/D ratio
of 66, sufficient for fully developed conditions. Working fluid: water
at 23.8 degC (kinematic viscosity approximately 1.0e-6 m^2/s).

Flow conditions:
- Bulk velocity: 1.72 m/s
- Reynolds number: Re = 152329
- Volume flow rate: 13.95 L/s
- Inlet pressure: 198 kPa (gauge)
- Outlet pressure: 162 kPa (gauge)
- Pressure drop: 180.2 kPa over the test section

The pressure drop of 80313 Pa corresponds to a Darcy friction
factor of f = 0.0351, in good agreement with the Moody diagram
prediction. Wall shear stress was estimated at 55.4 Pa using the
relation tau_w = (delta_P * D) / (4 * L).

PIV measurements in a cross-sectional plane at x/D = 114 revealed
the expected logarithmic velocity profile. The centerline velocity was
0.88 m/s (10.2 km/h).
Turbulence intensity at the centerline: 6.6%.
