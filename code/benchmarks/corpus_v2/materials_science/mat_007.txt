Creep Test Report: Inconel 718, Sample CR-8007

Test conditions:
- Temperature: 658 degC
- Applied stress: 619 MPa
- Environment: Argon atmosphere at 99 kPa

Specimen: cylindrical, diameter 7.0 mm, gauge length
39 mm. The specimen was solution treated at 980 degC
for 1 h followed by double aging (720 degC).

Results:
- Primary creep duration: 37 h
- Minimum creep rate: 1.23e-08 s^{-1}
- Rupture time: 4435 h
- Elongation at rupture: 20.0%

The steady-state creep rate of 7.71e-08 s^{-1} is consistent
with diffusion-controlled dislocation climb mechanisms. Activation energy was
estimated at 284 kJ/mol via Arrhenius analysis across
three temperatures. Grain boundary cavitation was observed in post-test metallography.
The applied stress of 558700 kPa exceeds the threshold
for power-law breakdown. Specimen mass before test: 46.3 g;
after test: 39.1 g (minor oxidation loss).
