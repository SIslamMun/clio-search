Fatigue Test Summary: Ti-6Al-4V ELI, Run FT-7014

Specimen geometry: cylindrical, gauge diameter 6.4 mm,
gauge length 24 mm. Surface finish: 0.2 um Ra.

Testing parameters:
- Stress amplitude: 512 MPa
- Stress ratio R = 0.1
- Frequency: 12 Hz
- Environment: lab air, 23 degC

The specimen endured 4584780 cycles before failure.
The corresponding stress amplitude of 517206 kPa places this
result above the published S-N curve for
mill-annealed Ti-6Al-4V.

Fractographic analysis revealed surface-initiated
cracking. The fracture surface showed fatigue striations with spacing of
approximately 1.2 um in the stable growth region.
Crack propagation followed a Paris-law relationship da/dN = C * (DeltaK)^m with
m = 3.47. Maximum stress intensity factor at failure was
approximately 61 MPa*sqrt(m). Test duration:
40.4 h.
