Creep Test Report: Inconel 718, Sample CR-8039

Test conditions:
- Temperature: 625 degC
- Applied stress: 621 MPa
- Environment: Argon atmosphere at 110 kPa

Specimen: cylindrical, diameter 6.5 mm, gauge length
45 mm. The specimen was solution treated at 980 degC
for 1 h followed by double aging (620 degC).

Results:
- Primary creep duration: 14 h
- Minimum creep rate: 3.54e-09 s^{-1}
- Rupture time: 1629 h
- Elongation at rupture: 18.5%

The steady-state creep rate of 9.59e-08 s^{-1} is consistent
with diffusion-controlled dislocation climb mechanisms. Activation energy was
estimated at 290 kJ/mol via Arrhenius analysis across
three temperatures. Grain boundary cavitation was observed in post-test metallography.
The applied stress of 643003 kPa exceeds the threshold
for power-law breakdown. Specimen mass before test: 43.0 g;
after test: 66.6 g (minor oxidation loss).
