Fatigue Test Summary: Ti-6Al-4V ELI, Run FT-7006

Specimen geometry: cylindrical, gauge diameter 6.6 mm,
gauge length 22 mm. Surface finish: 0.5 um Ra.

Testing parameters:
- Stress amplitude: 467 MPa
- Stress ratio R = 0
- Frequency: 14 Hz
- Environment: lab air, 25 degC

The specimen endured 4324216 cycles before failure.
The corresponding stress amplitude of 448703 kPa places this
result above the published S-N curve for
mill-annealed Ti-6Al-4V.

Fractographic analysis revealed surface-initiated
cracking. The fracture surface showed fatigue striations with spacing of
approximately 1.2 um in the stable growth region.
Crack propagation followed a Paris-law relationship da/dN = C * (DeltaK)^m with
m = 2.82. Maximum stress intensity factor at failure was
approximately 84 MPa*sqrt(m). Test duration:
59.8 h.
