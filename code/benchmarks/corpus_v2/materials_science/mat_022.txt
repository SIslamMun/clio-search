Fatigue Test Summary: Ti-6Al-4V ELI, Run FT-7022

Specimen geometry: cylindrical, gauge diameter 6.1 mm,
gauge length 21 mm. Surface finish: 0.5 um Ra.

Testing parameters:
- Stress amplitude: 358 MPa
- Stress ratio R = 0
- Frequency: 28 Hz
- Environment: lab air, 24 degC

The specimen endured 384662 cycles before failure.
The corresponding stress amplitude of 529785 kPa places this
result above the published S-N curve for
mill-annealed Ti-6Al-4V.

Fractographic analysis revealed surface-initiated
cracking. The fracture surface showed fatigue striations with spacing of
approximately 3.5 um in the stable growth region.
Crack propagation followed a Paris-law relationship da/dN = C * (DeltaK)^m with
m = 3.21. Maximum stress intensity factor at failure was
approximately 78 MPa*sqrt(m). Test duration:
87.5 h.
