Creep Test Report: Inconel 718, Sample CR-8031

Test conditions:
- Temperature: 703 degC
- Applied stress: 679 MPa
- Environment: Argon atmosphere at 114 kPa

Specimen: cylindrical, diameter 9.5 mm, gauge length
44 mm. The specimen was solution treated at 980 degC
for 1 h followed by double aging (720 degC).

Results:
- Primary creep duration: 17 h
- Minimum creep rate: 9.58e-08 s^{-1}
- Rupture time: 2638 h
- Elongation at rupture: 6.0%

The steady-state creep rate of 2.57e-08 s^{-1} is consistent
with diffusion-controlled dislocation climb mechanisms. Activation energy was
estimated at 335 kJ/mol via Arrhenius analysis across
three temperatures. Grain boundary cavitation was observed in post-test metallography.
The applied stress of 591292 kPa exceeds the threshold
for power-law breakdown. Specimen mass before test: 72.1 g;
after test: 65.7 g (minor oxidation loss).
