Tensile Test Report: Sample MS-1000

A standard tensile test was performed on a low-carbon steel specimen (ASTM A36)
with initial gauge length of 51.4 mm and cross-sectional
area of 12.1 mm x 3.55 mm. The specimen
was loaded at a constant crosshead speed of 1.9 mm/min,
corresponding to a nominal strain rate of 0.0016 s^{-1}.

Results: The yield strength was 267.1 MPa (0.2% offset method)
and the ultimate tensile strength reached 509.2 MPa at
an engineering strain of 18.9%. The specimen fractured after
196 s of loading. Reduction in area was 55.4%.

The elastic modulus was calculated from the linear portion of the stress-strain
curve as 198 GPa, consistent with published values for
mild steel. The fracture surface exhibited a cup-and-cone morphology typical
of ductile failure. All measurements comply with ASTM E8/E8M-21 standards.
Sample thickness was 4.01 mm measured with a micrometer
having resolution of 0.01 mm. The applied force was computed as F = m * a
from Newton's second law, where the crosshead acceleration was used to
verify the load cell calibration (F = ma).
