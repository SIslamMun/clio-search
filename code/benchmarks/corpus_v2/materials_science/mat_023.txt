Creep Test Report: Inconel 718, Sample CR-8023

Test conditions:
- Temperature: 746 degC
- Applied stress: 650 MPa
- Environment: Argon atmosphere at 117 kPa

Specimen: cylindrical, diameter 6.9 mm, gauge length
29 mm. The specimen was solution treated at 980 degC
for 1 h followed by double aging (720 degC).

Results:
- Primary creep duration: 45 h
- Minimum creep rate: 3.45e-09 s^{-1}
- Rupture time: 3709 h
- Elongation at rupture: 11.6%

The steady-state creep rate of 9.32e-08 s^{-1} is consistent
with diffusion-controlled dislocation climb mechanisms. Activation energy was
estimated at 330 kJ/mol via Arrhenius analysis across
three temperatures. Grain boundary cavitation was observed in post-test metallography.
The applied stress of 672813 kPa exceeds the threshold
for power-law breakdown. Specimen mass before test: 72.4 g;
after test: 49.7 g (minor oxidation loss).
