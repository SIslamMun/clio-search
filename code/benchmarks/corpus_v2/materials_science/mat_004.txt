High-Strength Steel Evaluation: DP800 Dual-Phase

Specimen ID: HSS-5004
Sheet thickness: 1.70 mm
Rolling direction: longitudinal

This dual-phase steel was tested at multiple strain rates to characterize its
rate sensitivity. A servo-hydraulic machine was used for dynamic tests, while
quasi-static tests used a screw-driven frame.

Quasi-static results (strain rate 0.0072 s^{-1}):
- Yield strength: 457 MPa
- UTS: 824 MPa
- Uniform elongation: 15.2%

Dynamic results (strain rate 198 s^{-1}):
- Yield strength: 619 MPa
- UTS: 885 MPa

The stress increase from quasi-static to dynamic loading confirms positive strain
rate sensitivity typical of ferritic-martensitic microstructures. Sample preparation
involved EDM cutting with 0.29 mm wire, followed by
surface grinding to 1.4 um Ra finish. All pressures in
the hydraulic system were maintained at 19.5 MPa during
gripping. The test fixture was calibrated using a 66 kN
reference load cell. Total test time: 4.0 min.
