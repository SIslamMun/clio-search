Tensile Test Report: Sample MS-1008

A standard tensile test was performed on a low-carbon steel specimen (ASTM A36)
with initial gauge length of 52.2 mm and cross-sectional
area of 13.4 mm x 4.94 mm. The specimen
was loaded at a constant crosshead speed of 4.0 mm/min,
corresponding to a nominal strain rate of 0.0013 s^{-1}.

Results: The yield strength was 244.3 MPa (0.2% offset method)
and the ultimate tensile strength reached 482.5 MPa at
an engineering strain of 26.4%. The specimen fractured after
211 s of loading. Reduction in area was 58.0%.

The elastic modulus was calculated from the linear portion of the stress-strain
curve as 201 GPa, consistent with published values for
mild steel. The fracture surface exhibited a cup-and-cone morphology typical
of ductile failure. All measurements comply with ASTM E8/E8M-21 standards.
Sample thickness was 3.32 mm measured with a micrometer
having resolution of 0.01 mm. The applied force was computed as F = m * a
from Newton's second law, where the crosshead acceleration was used to
verify the load cell calibration (F = ma).
