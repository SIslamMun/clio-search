Fatigue Test Summary: Ti-6Al-4V ELI, Run FT-7038

Specimen geometry: cylindrical, gauge diameter 7.0 mm,
gauge length 23 mm. Surface finish: 0.6 um Ra.

Testing parameters:
- Stress amplitude: 501 MPa
- Stress ratio R = -1
- Frequency: 14 Hz
- Environment: lab air, 20 degC

The specimen endured 1328275 cycles before failure.
The corresponding stress amplitude of 397709 kPa places this
result below the published S-N curve for
mill-annealed Ti-6Al-4V.

Fractographic analysis revealed surface-initiated
cracking. The fracture surface showed fatigue striations with spacing of
approximately 3.0 um in the stable growth region.
Crack propagation followed a Paris-law relationship da/dN = C * (DeltaK)^m with
m = 2.94. Maximum stress intensity factor at failure was
approximately 85 MPa*sqrt(m). Test duration:
76.8 h.
