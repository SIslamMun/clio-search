Fatigue Test Summary: Ti-6Al-4V ELI, Run FT-7030

Specimen geometry: cylindrical, gauge diameter 7.4 mm,
gauge length 16 mm. Surface finish: 0.2 um Ra.

Testing parameters:
- Stress amplitude: 547 MPa
- Stress ratio R = 0.1
- Frequency: 27 Hz
- Environment: lab air, 20 degC

The specimen endured 3526583 cycles before failure.
The corresponding stress amplitude of 527227 kPa places this
result near the published S-N curve for
mill-annealed Ti-6Al-4V.

Fractographic analysis revealed surface-initiated
cracking. The fracture surface showed fatigue striations with spacing of
approximately 3.7 um in the stable growth region.
Crack propagation followed a Paris-law relationship da/dN = C * (DeltaK)^m with
m = 3.27. Maximum stress intensity factor at failure was
approximately 70 MPa*sqrt(m). Test duration:
15.2 h.
