Material Characterization: Al-6061-T6 Sheet, Batch 2001

This report documents the mechanical properties of aluminum alloy 6061-T6 sheet
stock received from supplier. Sheet thickness: 1.55 mm.
Three dog-bone specimens were laser-cut per ASTM E8 with gauge width 6.4 mm
and gauge length 28 mm.

Average tensile properties (n=3):
- Yield strength (Rp0.2): 291.8 MPa
- Ultimate tensile strength: 321.0 MPa
- Elongation at break: 14.9%
- Young's modulus: 71.2 GPa

The stress at 270 MPa was recorded as the onset of plastic
deformation. Hardness measurements using Vickers (HV10) gave 113 HV,
consistent with the T6 temper condition. Tests were conducted at 24.1 degC
ambient temperature. The strain was monitored using a clip-on extensometer with
34 mm gauge length, sampling at 73 Hz.

Duration per test: approximately 7.8 min. The load cell capacity
was 100 kN, and the machine compliance was corrected in post-processing.
The force on the specimen follows F = m * a (Newton's second law) during
dynamic loading phases; static equilibrium was confirmed (F = ma check).
