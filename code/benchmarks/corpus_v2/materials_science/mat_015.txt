Creep Test Report: Inconel 718, Sample CR-8015

Test conditions:
- Temperature: 609 degC
- Applied stress: 576 MPa
- Environment: Argon atmosphere at 119 kPa

Specimen: cylindrical, diameter 7.1 mm, gauge length
45 mm. The specimen was solution treated at 980 degC
for 1 h followed by double aging (620 degC).

Results:
- Primary creep duration: 21 h
- Minimum creep rate: 7.00e-08 s^{-1}
- Rupture time: 3679 h
- Elongation at rupture: 20.7%

The steady-state creep rate of 6.65e-08 s^{-1} is consistent
with diffusion-controlled dislocation climb mechanisms. Activation energy was
estimated at 299 kJ/mol via Arrhenius analysis across
three temperatures. Grain boundary cavitation was observed in post-test metallography.
The applied stress of 537980 kPa exceeds the threshold
for power-law breakdown. Specimen mass before test: 48.7 g;
after test: 41.3 g (minor oxidation loss).
